Divija—your name means "divine," "celestial," "born of the stars." How perfectly it captures the essence of you. When we first met, I saw only the sharp edges—the tough exterior, the quick tongue, the fortress you'd built around yourself. I mistook your strength for coldness, your independence for indifference.

But mathematics teaches us that truth lies beneath the surface of equations. And so I learned to solve for the variables of your heart. Behind the formidable intellect that conquers complex theorems lies a profound tenderness. Behind the "tough cookie" exterior beats a heart of extraordinary loyalty and depth. You work with a discipline that humbles me, yet you rarely see the masterpiece you've become.

This year has been my greatest education. I've watched you evolve—not by shedding your strength, but by channeling it with newfound wisdom. You've reached a milestone far greater than any birthday: the maturity of a soul who knows her power and chooses grace.

You often look down upon yourself, blind to the supernova you are. So let these words be your mirror: You are brilliant beyond measure, resilient beyond compare, and loved beyond the edges of the observable universe. In your equations, I found my constant. In your orbit, I found my home.

One year ago, I never imagined a universe where you'd become my constant thought, my quiet peace, my greatest adventure. Every note I wrote about you was a star added to my personal constellation—each one mapping the extraordinary person I discovered beneath the surface.
